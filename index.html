Web Design

Combining site architecture, code, and visual fireworks.

browarc.com

I was tasked with revising our old website into modern design standards and something a little more practical for our customers. I needed something clean, easy-to-update, and fully mobile-friendly. Enter Squarespace.

It is not the most hands-on approach, but I needed something quick, guaranteed, and easily update-able. Squarespace hit all of these criteria, plus it is a joy to use!

I started with our user: mostly women on their cell phones in the mall. So it had to be super mobile-friendly (another plus for Squarespace). Our locations needed to be featured, so I made that the call-out button on the navbar. We also needed to accentuate what we did, any updates and deals, and how to contact us. Those are the main pillars upon which the website is hung.

After the frame was built out, I added in images, font, and styling. Overall the feedback has been immensely positive, and we have had close to 30,000 pageviews!

Building out a website is just the beginning: I continually promote our website, analyze user behavior with Google Analytics (who's looking, where are the going on the website, how long is it taking them), and updating it with more images, information, and news!

And of course, there's this website. I created this using a modified template. Yes it's a little Bootstrap-y but it gets the job done. I wanted a place to showcase my skills and give a little peak into my personality.

And this is just the beginning: I plan to continually update this site with updates, newest works, and more and more features!

My Coding Journey

How I taught myself am teaching myself how to code

How did iiiii get these lil old coding skillz?

OK, let me tell you…

Took an HTML online class for 99 dollars in 2005. Didn’t know there was more to it than that, didn’t want to pay more 99 dollars. Wanted to become a web designer/graphic designer, but decided to become a teacher instead. I have no idea why. I’m dumb.

Taught high school history for three years. It was amazing and terrible. I learned a lot about organization, responsibility, and USER EXPERIENCE. Designing a UX all day, every day for hostile, angry, lethargic teenagers is probably the best in-person focus group possible.

Helped run a chain of salons for 5 years. Learned a lot about organization, marketing, and working at a computer all day (this is a learned skill). Designed the UX for an interconnected, absolutely-must-work, nationwide POS system. It is used by our huge variety of employees: young and old, computer literate and illiterate, English speakers and non-English speakers. This is probably the best user testing group possible.

I also tried to make our first website and it wasn’t too hot. Hired a company to do this, and helped them with the UX, UI, design, and copy. Learned a lot about social media and email marketing. Can never know enough about those. Redid our first website using Squarespace, designing all the UX, design, and copy. Launching this was one of the most exciting days of my life. Now working on a second website, and my own website, and some other projects too. Coming soon :)
